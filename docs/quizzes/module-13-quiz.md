## Question 18

*Lesson L04 · Fiscal Policy*


A balanced budget (ΔG=ΔT=$50bn) with b=0.8 m=0.1. What is the change in Y?


**Type:** Calculation

??? success "Show Answer"
    **Answer:** $50bn

    ΔY = k_G×ΔG + k_T×(-ΔT) = (1/0.3)×50 + (-0.8/0.3)×(-50) = 166.7 - 133.3 = $50bn (balanced budget multiplier=1)

---

## Question 19

*Lesson L04 · Fiscal Policy*


Explain why the balanced budget multiplier equals 1.


**Type:** Short Answer

??? success "Show Answer"
    **Answer:** Spending increases Y by k_G×ΔG while tax increase reduces Y by k_T×ΔT. Net effect = (k_G - |k_T|)×ΔG = 1 since k_G - |k_T| = 1/(1-b+m) - b/(1-b+m) = (1-b)/(1-b+m) ≈ 1 when m is small.

    The government spending effect always exceeds the tax drag by exactly ΔG.

## Question 23

If actual deficit is 2% of GDP and the output gap is -3% with sensitivity 0.5 what is the structural deficit?


**Type:** Calculation

??? success "Show Answer"
    **Answer:** 0.5%

    Structural deficit = actual deficit - (sensitivity × output gap) = 2% - (0.5×(-3%)) = 2% - (-1.5%) = 0.5%
